=== CORE RELATIONSHIPS ===

Harry James Potter
Magical Aptitude: Exceptionally strong defensive magic, instinctive dueling ability, high resilience to Dark magic, advanced Patronus mastery at young age, strong wand affinity.
Relationships:
James Potter: Father. Inherited courage, instinctive leadership, and natural talent in combat magic.
Lily Evans Potter: Mother. Source of sacrificial protection magic that defined Harry’s survival and magical destiny.
Albus Dumbledore: Mentor and strategist. Relationship built on trust mixed with manipulation for the greater good.
Severus Snape: Protector from shadows. Relationship defined by misunderstanding; Snape’s protection stemmed from Lily, not Harry himself.
Hermione Granger: Intellectual anchor. She compensated for Harry’s impulsiveness and filled strategic gaps.
Ron Weasley: Emotional anchor and battlefield partner. Represented loyalty and grounding humanity.
Voldemort: Mirror antagonist. Shared wand core, shared destiny, opposite philosophies regarding death and power.

Hermione Jean Granger
Magical Aptitude: Elite theoretical knowledge, advanced spell precision, rapid learning curve, exceptional memory, high magical stamina.
Relationships:
Harry Potter: Strategic equal and moral compass. Often the unseen architect behind victories.
Ron Weasley: Emotional partner. Their bond balanced logic and intuition.
Ministry of Magic: Later institutional authority. Transitioned from rebel to reformer.
House-elves: Advocacy relationship rooted in justice rather than sentimentality.

Ronald Bilius Weasley
Magical Aptitude: Tactical intuition, situational awareness, high emotional intelligence in combat, exceptional chess-based strategy.
Relationships:
Harry Potter: Brother-in-arms. Shared survival experiences forged unbreakable loyalty.
Hermione Granger: Growth catalyst. Pushed Ron beyond insecurity into self-worth.
Weasley Family: Moral foundation and emotional safety net.
Horcrux Locket: Antagonistic psychological relationship exposing deepest fears.

=== DUMBLEDORE ERA ===

Albus Dumbledore
Magical Aptitude: Legendary spell mastery, advanced transfiguration, alchemy expertise, strategic foresight, deep understanding of magical theory.
Relationships:
Gellert Grindelwald: Former intellectual equal and emotional attachment. Their bond shaped Dumbledore’s lifelong fear of power.
Harry Potter: Chosen successor figure. Relationship mixed mentorship, secrecy, and moral burden.
Severus Snape: Trusted operative. Relationship built on mutual understanding of sacrifice.
The Elder Wand: Symbolic temptation rather than tool of dominance.

Severus Snape
Magical Aptitude: Genius-level potion-making, spell invention, advanced Occlumency and Legilimency, high Dark Arts resistance.
Relationships:
Lily Evans: Emotional core. Entire life trajectory altered by this bond.
James Potter: Rivalry and trauma source. Fueled bitterness and insecurity.
Dumbledore: Moral contract. Loyalty maintained through guilt and love.
Harry Potter: Proxy relationship. Protected him while resenting his resemblance to James.

Minerva McGonagall
Magical Aptitude: Master-level Transfiguration, Animagus control, battlefield command magic.
Relationships:
Dumbledore: Loyal deputy and executor of his ideals.
Students: Authority tempered by deep protectiveness.
Hogwarts: Symbolic guardian of institutional integrity.

=== THE DARK AXIS ===

Tom Marvolo Riddle (Voldemort)
Magical Aptitude: Extraordinary raw power, advanced Dark magic, Horcrux creation, Parseltongue mastery, charisma-based influence.
Relationships:
Harry Potter: Existential enemy. Saw Harry as proof of his own mortality.
Bellatrix Lestrange: Fanatical follower. Relationship based on devotion, not equality.
Death Eaters: Tools rather than allies. Loyalty enforced by fear.
Horcruxes: Pathological attachment representing fear of death.

Bellatrix Lestrange
Magical Aptitude: Elite duelist, Dark curse mastery, high pain tolerance, unstable but powerful magic output.
Relationships:
Voldemort: Object of worship. Identity fully subsumed under loyalty.
Narcissa Malfoy: Sisterly bond strained by ideology.
Neville Longbottom: Indirect antagonist through parental trauma.

Draco Malfoy
Magical Aptitude: Above-average spellwork, aptitude for Occlumency under pressure, limited emotional resilience.
Relationships:
Lucius Malfoy: Burdening legacy. Source of fear and expectation.
Voldemort: Coercive authority figure. Relationship defined by terror.
Harry Potter: Rival and mirror of alternate upbringing.
Astoria Greengrass: Stabilizing post-war influence.

Dolores Umbridge
Magical Aptitude: Limited raw power, high legal-magic manipulation, psychological coercion.
Relationships:
Ministry of Magic: Weaponized bureaucracy.
Students: Subjects of control rather than education.
Centaurs: Mutual hostility rooted in arrogance.

=== SUMMARY INSIGHT ===

Power in the wizarding world does not correlate strictly with raw magic.
The most decisive victories emerged from emotional bonds, moral choices, and willingness to sacrifice.
Characters who pursued domination fractured themselves.
Those who accepted love, loss, and limitation endured.
