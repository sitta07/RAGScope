Source: The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1-7
Category: Magic Spells & Charms

Expelliarmus (The Disarming Charm)
Description: A foundational defensive charm designed to neutralize an opponent without causing lasting harm. While commonly associated with dueling etiquette, its simplicity masks deep versatility.
Incantation: Ex-pel-ee-AR-mus
Light: Scarlet
Effect: Forces the target to release whatever they are holding, most often a wand. When cast with sufficient power or emotional intent, it can physically knock an opponent backward or shatter weaker enchantments.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Repeated use of Expelliarmus became a recognizable magical “signature,” allowing experienced observers to identify Harry Potter during battles.
The spell can disarm multiple opponents simultaneously if cast with wide intent and sufficient power.
Symbolically associated with defensive morality, favoring protection and restraint over domination.

Expecto Patronum (The Patronus Charm)
Description: One of the most advanced defensive charms taught at Hogwarts, requiring focused happiness, emotional resilience, and mental clarity. It manifests protective positive energy.
Incantation: Ex-pek-toh pa-TRO-num
Light: Silver
Effect: Conjures a Patronus, either corporeal (animal form) or non-corporeal (mist-like). Fully corporeal Patronuses can repel Dementors, Lethifolds, and certain Dark enchantments.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
The Patronus form reflects core personality traits, not surface preferences.
A Patronus may change form after profound emotional shifts, as seen with Nymphadora Tonks and Severus Snape.
Very few witches and wizards can cast this charm reliably; mastery is considered Auror-level skill.

Avada Kedavra (The Killing Curse)
Description: One of the three Unforgivable Curses. Requires strong intent to kill; casual casting will fail.
Incantation: A-VA-da ke-DA-vra
Light: Blinding Green
Effect: Causes immediate death by forcibly severing the soul from the body. Leaves no physical damage.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
The curse cannot be blocked by magical shields, only by physical obstacles or sacrificial protection.
Repeated use visibly deforms the caster’s soul.
Harry Potter’s survival was due to sacrificial protection and wand core resonance, not resistance to the spell itself.

Wingardium Leviosa (The Levitation Charm)
Description: A basic but essential charm allowing objects to defy gravity. Often underestimated due to its early introduction.
Incantation: Win-GAR-dee-um Lev-ee-OH-sa
Light: None or faint blue shimmer
Effect: Causes objects to levitate and move according to wand motion. Precision improves with wrist control rather than force.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Incorrect pronunciation destabilizes the charm.
Used in advanced spellwork as a base layer for flight-related enchantments.
Combined with other charms to construct complex moving systems within Hogwarts.

Sectumsempra
Description: A highly dangerous dark curse developed by Severus Snape for personal defense during his school years.
Effect: Inflicts deep, violent slashing wounds as if cut by an invisible blade.
Counter-curse: Vulnera Sanentur
Advanced Notes and Lore:
The curse responds to emotional aggression and hostility.
Standard healing spells are ineffective; only the specific counter-curse can close the wounds.
Its invention demonstrates Snape’s early mastery of spell creation.

Protego (The Shield Charm)
Description: A defensive charm that conjures a magical barrier to deflect spells and physical objects.
Incantation: Pro-TAY-go
Light: Translucent shimmer
Effect: Blocks or deflects minor to moderate spells depending on caster strength.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Variants include Protego Maxima and Protego Totalum for area-wide protection.
Cannot block Unforgivable Curses.
Effectiveness depends heavily on reaction speed and intent.

Lumos / Nox
Description: Basic illumination charm and its counter.
Incantation: LOO-mos / Nox
Light: White
Effect: Produces light from wand tip; Nox extinguishes it.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Brightness can be modulated subconsciously.
Useful for detecting hidden enchantments and magical residue.
Prolonged Lumos use may reveal the caster’s location in stealth situations.

Petrificus Totalus (The Full Body-Bind Curse)
Description: A curse that immobilizes the target completely.
Incantation: Pe-TRI-fi-cus To-TAH-lus
Light: None or pale gray flash
Effect: Paralyzes the body while leaving consciousness intact.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Often used in ambush or escape situations.
Long-term exposure can cause muscle trauma if not released properly.
Countered by Finite Incantatem or manual reversal.

Accio (The Summoning Charm)
Description: Draws an object toward the caster regardless of distance, provided the caster knows its location.
Incantation: AK-see-oh
Light: None
Effect: Causes target object to fly into the caster’s hand.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Requires mental clarity and spatial awareness.
Can summon objects through walls and obstacles.
Overuse may result in magical fatigue due to spatial strain.

Obliviate (The Memory Charm)
Description: Alters, removes, or suppresses memories.
Incantation: Oh-BLI-vee-ate
Light: Pale green or white
Effect: Erases or modifies specific memories.
Advanced Notes and Lore:
Precision determines whether memories are altered cleanly or damaged.
Improper use can cause permanent cognitive impairment.
Widely used by the Ministry to maintain the Statute of Secrecy.

Magic taught in classrooms is only the surface layer. True mastery emerges when intention, emotion, ethics, and control align. Spells do not merely obey words; they respond to the wizard behind the wand.
