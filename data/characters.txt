Source: Ministry of Magic Archives & Hogwarts Student Records
Category: Biographies & Profiles

=== THE GOLDEN TRIO ===

Harry James Potter
Title: The Boy Who Lived, The Chosen One
House: Gryffindor
Patronus: Stag
Wand: 11" Holly, Phoenix feather core
Bio: The only known survivor of the Killing Curse (Avada Kedavra), protected by sacrificial magic cast through Lily Potter’s willing death. Son of James and Lily Potter, both members of the original Order of the Phoenix. Marked as Voldemort’s equal due to the prophecy spoken by Sybill Trelawney. Unknowingly carried a fragment of Voldemort’s soul, making him a living Horcrux. Defeated Lord Voldemort in 1998 at the Battle of Hogwarts by exploiting wand allegiance laws rather than raw magical force. Inherited the Invisibility Cloak from Ignotus Peverell’s bloodline and temporarily possessed all three Deathly Hallows, though he chose to relinquish their power. Later became Head of the Auror Office and reformed the department’s focus on prevention and rehabilitation.

Hermione Jean Granger
Title: The Brightest Witch of Her Age
House: Gryffindor
Patronus: Otter
Wand: 10¾" Vine wood, Dragon heartstring core
Bio: Muggle-born witch whose intellect, discipline, and moral clarity defined the success of the resistance against Voldemort. Exhibited near-photographic memory and exceptional aptitude for spellcraft, theory, and magical law. Used a Time-Turner responsibly at age thirteen, an unprecedented trust by the Ministry. Modified her parents’ memories to protect them during the war, demonstrating emotional sacrifice equal to her intellect. Founded S.P.E.W. to challenge systemic magical inequality. Instrumental in Horcrux research, enchantment-breaking, and battlefield logistics. Later became Minister for Magic, overseeing major reforms in Muggle-born rights, house-elf protections, and judicial transparency.

Ronald Bilius Weasley
House: Gryffindor
Patronus: Jack Russell Terrier
Wand: 14" Willow, Unicorn hair core (his second wand)
Bio: Pure-blood wizard from a large, economically modest family, giving him deep insight into wizarding social dynamics. Known for emotional intelligence, loyalty, and instinctive strategic thinking, particularly under pressure. His sacrifice during the chess match guarding the Philosopher’s Stone demonstrated early courage. Temporarily abandoned the Horcrux hunt due to the locket’s psychological influence but returned of his own volition, symbolizing personal growth. Destroyed Salazar Slytherin’s locket Horcrux by confronting his deepest insecurities. Later joined George Weasley in expanding Weasleys’ Wizard Wheezes before entering Auror service, specializing in field operations.

=== HOGWARTS STAFF ===

Albus Percival Wulfric Brian Dumbledore
Role: Headmaster of Hogwarts
House: Gryffindor
Patronus: Phoenix
Bio: Widely regarded as the greatest wizard of modern times, though he considered wisdom born from regret his true strength. Brilliant theoretician and innovator, credited with discovering the twelve uses of dragon’s blood. Defeated Gellert Grindelwald in 1945, ending a global magical threat born from their shared youthful ideology. Served multiple times as Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot and was repeatedly offered the position of Minister for Magic, which he declined out of self-awareness regarding his attraction to power. Orchestrated a long-term strategy against Voldemort, including the deliberate sacrifice of his own life to preserve Draco Malfoy’s soul and neutralize the Elder Wand’s lineage, though unforeseen wand allegiance complications arose.

Severus Snape
Role: Potions Master, Defence Against the Dark Arts Professor, Headmaster
House: Slytherin
Patronus: Doe
Bio: Exceptionally gifted in potion-making and spell creation, improving upon standard textbooks while still a student. Invented several advanced spells, including Sectumsempra, under the alias “The Half-Blood Prince.” Former Death Eater who defected after Voldemort targeted Lily Evans, the woman he loved since childhood. Served as a double agent at immense personal cost, enduring hatred and suspicion from both sides. His Patronus permanently changed to mirror Lily’s, reflecting emotional constancy rather than redemption through absolution. Killed by Nagini under Voldemort’s mistaken belief regarding the Elder Wand’s allegiance. Posthumously recognized as one of the war’s most critical figures.

Minerva McGonagall
Role: Transfiguration Professor, Head of Gryffindor House, Headmistress
House: Gryffindor
Patronus: Cat
Bio: Renowned master of Transfiguration and a registered Animagus capable of transforming into a tabby cat. Educated, disciplined, and uncompromising in matters of integrity, yet deeply protective of her students. Served loyally under Dumbledore and later assumed leadership of Hogwarts during its most fragile recovery period. Commanded the castle’s defenses during the Battle of Hogwarts, including the activation of ancient protective enchantments. Known for dry wit and moral clarity, balancing compassion with authority.

Rubeus Hagrid
Role: Keeper of Keys and Grounds, Care of Magical Creatures Professor
House: Gryffindor (Expelled in 3rd year)
Bio: Half-giant wizard with exceptional physical resilience and emotional gentleness. Expelled after being falsely accused by Tom Riddle of opening the Chamber of Secrets, resulting in a broken wand concealed within an umbrella. Despite limited formal education, possessed extensive practical knowledge of magical creatures. Introduced students to misunderstood species, often challenging Ministry classifications. A founding member of both iterations of the Order of the Phoenix and one of Dumbledore’s most trusted allies.

=== THE ANTAGONISTS ===

Tom Marvolo Riddle (Lord Voldemort)
Title: The Dark Lord, He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named
House: Slytherin
Wand: 13½" Yew, Phoenix feather core (brother to Harry Potter’s wand)
Bio: The most dangerous Dark Wizard in recorded history, driven by fear of death and obsession with blood purity despite being a half-blood himself. Born from a union manipulated by love potion, contributing to his emotional incapacity for love. Exceptionally charismatic and intellectually brilliant as a student, concealing cruelty beneath charm. Created multiple Horcruxes through calculated murder, fragmenting his soul beyond natural limits. Misinterpreted prophecy and wand lore repeatedly, leading to his downfall. Died when his Killing Curse rebounded during the final duel at Hogwarts.

Bellatrix Lestrange
House: Slytherin
Bio: Fanatically loyal follower of Voldemort, deriving pleasure from cruelty and chaos. A highly skilled duelist with advanced proficiency in Dark Arts. Instrumental in enforcing Voldemort’s regime through terror. Personally tortured Frank and Alice Longbottom into permanent insanity. Murdered Sirius Black in the Department of Mysteries. Ultimately killed by Molly Weasley during the Battle of Hogwarts after underestimating maternal rage.

Draco Lucius Malfoy
House: Slytherin
Wand: 10" Hawthorn, Unicorn hair core
Bio: Raised within pure-blood supremacist ideology, shaped more by fear and expectation than conviction. Exhibited talent in spellwork and Occlumency under duress. Forced into Death Eater service as punishment for his father’s failures. Tasked with assassinating Dumbledore but proved psychologically unable to commit murder. His hesitation repeatedly altered the war’s trajectory. Later withdrew from extremist ideology, choosing a quieter life and raising his son, Scorpius, with greater tolerance.

Dolores Jane Umbridge
Role: High Inquisitor, Senior Undersecretary to the Minister
House: Slytherin
Bio: Embodiment of bureaucratic cruelty masquerading as order. Exercised authority through legal manipulation rather than raw magical power. Advocated blood purity policies while denying affiliation with Voldemort when convenient. Instituted abusive disciplinary measures at Hogwarts, including the use of blood quills. Oversaw Muggle-born Registration Commission during Voldemort’s regime. Universally despised across multiple magical species. Currently serving a life sentence in Azkaban.

=== THE MARAUDERS & ALLIES ===

Sirius Black (Padfoot)
House: Gryffindor
Form: Animagus (Black Dog)
Bio: Heir to the ancient House of Black who rejected his family’s supremacist ideology. Best friend of James Potter and godfather to Harry. Wrongly imprisoned in Azkaban for twelve years without trial due to Ministry negligence. Co-creator of the Marauder’s Map. Lived as a fugitive after escape, enduring psychological damage from imprisonment. Killed by Bellatrix Lestrange during the Battle of the Department of Mysteries.

Remus Lupin (Moony)
House: Gryffindor
Condition: Werewolf
Bio: Bitten as a child by Fenrir Greyback, leading to lifelong social stigma. Exceptionally compassionate and intellectually grounded. Served as Defence Against the Dark Arts professor, emphasizing practical survival over bravado. Helped design the Marauder’s Map. Struggled with self-worth and fear of burdening others. Married Nymphadora Tonks and became father to Teddy Lupin. Killed in the Battle of Hogwarts by Antonin Dolohov.

Neville Longbottom
House: Gryffindor
Bio: One of two children referenced in the prophecy, whose fate diverged due to Voldemort’s choice. Endured childhood trauma from parents’ torture and persistent self-doubt. Developed exceptional aptitude for Herbology and resistance leadership. Became a central figure in maintaining Dumbledore’s Army during Hogwarts’ occupation. Destroyed Nagini, the final Horcrux, using the Sword of Gryffindor. Later became a respected Herbology Professor.

Luna Lovegood
House: Ravenclaw
Bio: Known for unconventional beliefs and unfiltered honesty. Demonstrated emotional resilience following her mother’s death. Possessed unique perceptual openness, allowing her to see Thestrals early. Provided moral clarity and calm during periods of chaos. Loyal member of Dumbledore’s Army and participant in multiple battles. Daughter of Xenophilius Lovegood, editor of The Quibbler.

=== LEGENDS & OTHERS ===

Newt Scamander
House: Hufflepuff
Bio: Distinguished Magizoologist with deep empathy for magical creatures. Expelled briefly but reinstated due to Dumbledore’s intervention. Authored “Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them,” which became a standard Hogwarts textbook. Played a key role in opposing Grindelwald through non-violent resistance and creature-based strategies.

Dobby the House-Elf
Bio: Formerly enslaved by the Malfoy family under abusive conditions. Freed through a loophole exploited by Harry Potter, symbolizing resistance through cleverness rather than power. Demonstrated uncommon independence for a house-elf. Repeatedly risked his life to protect Harry and others. Died rescuing prisoners from Malfoy Manor. Epitaph reads: “Here Lies Dobby, A Free Elf.”

Garrick Ollivander
Role: Wandmaker
Bio: Member of a family whose wandmaking lineage dates back to 382 B.C. Possessed encyclopedic memory of wand lore and individual wand histories. Emphasized that wands choose the wizard, reflecting compatibility rather than dominance. Kidnapped and tortured by Voldemort for Elder Wand knowledge but survived to testify after the war.

Nicolas Flamel
Bio: Only known creator of the Philosopher’s Stone, granting immortality via the Elixir of Life. Lived over six centuries alongside his wife, Perenelle. Close friend of Dumbledore. Chose to destroy the Stone voluntarily, accepting mortality as a natural conclusion rather than a failure.

Gellert Grindelwald
Bio: One of history’s most formidable Dark Wizards, advocating wizard dominance under the guise of “the greater good.” Exceptionally persuasive and ideologically driven. Shared a profound intellectual and emotional bond with Albus Dumbledore in youth. Defeated in a legendary duel in 1945 and imprisoned in Nurmengard. Ultimately refused to assist Voldemort, indicating ideological divergence rather than redemption.
